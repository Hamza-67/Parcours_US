Commercial Strategy Redesign for a Student Engineering Consultancy

03 — AMJE Lille 2025–2026 · ongoing Business Manager mandate

Context

AMJE Lille (Arts et Métiers Junior-Entreprise) is the student consulting organization of ENSAM Lille. As Business Manager (Chargé d'affaires), I am responsible for business development: prospecting, qualification, proposal writing, and client follow-up.

Nine months of historical data revealed a conversion problem severe enough to constitute a structural issue, not a performance fluctuation. This document is the analytical response — a full funnel decomposition, root cause analysis, and redesigned commercial strategy.

The numbers: 286 prospects contacted → 7 studies signed. Conversion rate: 2.4%. Lost proposals: 31 740 € — 1.6× the realized revenue (~19 560 €). Note: the 13 lost proposals averaged 2 441 €/proposal vs. 2 794 € for signed contracts — consistent with smaller-scope clients having lower commitment and higher dropout rates.

Bottleneck: The critical drop is at the Response → Qualification stage (47.6% → 14.7%). 116 prospects responded but were never converted into a qualified conversation. This is a targeting and messaging problem, not a volume problem. Adding more cold calls would not fix a 14.7% qualification rate.

Five Structural Causes

#CauseEvidence
1 Unsegmented targeting 77.9% of prospects in a single sector (SOCE); no differentiated value proposition per segment
2 Over-reliance on cold calling 88% of outreach via phone; regulatory risk from RGPD B2B reforms (June/August 2026)
3 Message misalignment Pitch not adapted to client decision cycles (SME vs. corporate procurement timelines differ by 3–6 months)
4 No packaged offers All proposals custom-scoped from scratch; increases client decision friction and proposal writing time
5 Zero prescriber network No alumni referrals, no repeat client program, no partner JEs — despite AMJE network access

OR Mapping

The analytical structure of this project maps directly onto several OR frameworks:

  • Bottleneck analysis — the funnel decomposition is a direct application of the theory of constraints: the system throughput (signed studies) is limited by the weakest stage (qualification at 14.7%), not by total volume.
  • Multi-Armed Bandit (Thompson Sampling) — channel allocation across 4 arms (alumni / SME / startup / prescriber) with uncertain conversion rates per channel. As real conversion data accumulates, Thompson Sampling would optimally balance exploration vs. exploitation. Currently modeled analytically; live experiment planned for end of 2026.
  • Yield management — the three-tier pricing structure (Starter / Growth / Premium) mirrors airline fare class management: segment clients by willingness-to-pay and decision timeline, price accordingly, manage capacity (student hours) across segments.
  • Robust planning — the channel diversification away from cold calling is a direct response to regulatory risk (RGPD B2B reform, June/August 2026). This is robust optimization under uncertainty about regulatory constraint activation.

Case Study — TourniMap Mission

One consulting mission managed end-to-end as Business Manager: a commercial route optimization tool for a retail distribution client operating 650 points of sale across the Hérault department (southern France). Conducted in collaboration with MBS Junior Conseil.

What I handled, start to finish: identified the client need, won the contract through a competitive bid, negotiated terms and pricing, wrote the full technical specifications (cahier des charges) and quote, recruited and onboarded an external developer (intervenant), managed the delivery timeline, and supervised the final handoff.

The underlying problem — assigning 650 retail nodes to optimized routes across 13 geographic zones — is a Vehicle Routing Problem (VRP). Solved via K-Means++ clustering + 2-opt local search on OSRM road distances, deployed client-side on GitHub Pages. See the full technical write-up on the TourniMap project page →
