About

Two projects separated by 200 years of physics share the same skeleton. A flywheel optimized under stress constraints. A financial portfolio allocated under market uncertainty. Both reduce to the same question: how do you make the best decision when the model is never quite right?

I came from Morocco to study in France — prep class at Faidherbe, now engineering at ENSAM Lille. That move taught me something beyond mathematics: how to navigate unfamiliar systems, find the right people, and build without a safety net.

Alongside coursework, I founded ThePnLab (an algorithmic trading platform), manage commercial strategy at AMJE Lille, and contribute to research at LISPEN lab. Not scattered — all optimization, all decision-making, different domains.

The US comes next because that's where the serious work happens — in OR, in AI, in finance, and in the kind of entrepreneurship that turns a model into a company.
