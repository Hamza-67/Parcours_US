Formula Student — ENSAM Lille

05 — FSAM ENSAM Lille · 2025–2026 Project Management & Technical

Formula Student is one of the most demanding student engineering competitions in the world. Teams design, build, and race a single-seater formula car — judged on performance, cost, design, and business plan. My role sits at the intersection of engineering management and technical coordination.

Context

The ENSAM Lille FSAM team competes in Formula Student events across Europe — including FS Spain and FS Germany, two of the most prestigious stops on the circuit. The car is designed and built entirely by students, covering mechanical, electrical, aerodynamic, and embedded systems.

My involvement covers project management and technical support, with a focus on the structural organization of the project: knowing what parts exist, where they are, and what is missing at any given moment.

Bill of Materials — Full Vehicle BOM

The most complex deliverable of my mandate: a complete Bill of Materials covering the entire vehicle across 18+ distinct assemblies, built jointly in Excel and 3DExperience (Dassault Systèmes).

Each assembly is broken down to the individual part level: part number, description, supplier, quantity, unit, and stock status. The structure follows the vehicle's physical architecture — chassis, suspension, powertrain, braking, aerodynamics, electronics — enabling cross-team traceability.

Why this matters for OR: A Bill of Materials is structurally a directed acyclic graph — each node is a sub-assembly, each edge a dependency. Optimal procurement planning on a BOM is a multi-level lot-sizing problem (MLLS), solvable by integer programming. The Excel version I built is a manual approximation of what a formal model would automate.

Stock Management

Using the BOM as the single source of truth, I manage the team's physical inventory: tracking what has been received, what is missing, and how parts are organized in the workshop storage.

The workflow: cross-reference the BOM against physical stock, identify gaps, prioritize reorders based on assembly criticality, and organize parts in the local storage by assembly group. This is an ongoing process — the BOM evolves as the design is refined.

  • Track received vs. ordered vs. missing parts across all assemblies
  • Flag critical path parts — those blocking assembly start
  • Organize physical storage by assembly group for workshop efficiency
  • Update BOM in 3DExperience as design changes are validated

Project Vision & Cross-Team Coordination

Beyond the BOM and stock, my role involves maintaining a global view of the project — where each sub-team stands, what is blocking, and what the critical path to competition looks like.

Formula Student is as much a project management challenge as an engineering one. A student team of ~30 people, multiple sub-systems in parallel, a fixed competition date — the constraints are real, and the margin for misalignment is thin.

Honest Limitations

  • Car not yet completed — competition participation pending build completion
  • BOM maintained manually in Excel; no automated synchronization with 3DExperience
  • Stock tracking done without dedicated inventory software — spreadsheet-based
  • No formal critical path analysis (CPM/PERT) yet implemented — managed informally
